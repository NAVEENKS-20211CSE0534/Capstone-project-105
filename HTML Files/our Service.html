Garam Masala

A rich blend of spices that adds warmth and depth to dishes. It consists of ground cumin, coriander, black pepper, and cardamom, which create a well-rounded flavor profile. Used extensively in Indian cuisine, Garam masala is ideal for curries, stews, and rice dishes. It can be added at different stages of cooking for varying flavor impacts. Use it sparingly to allow the unique spice characteristics to shine through.

Sabji Masala

Perfectly crafted spices for seasoning vegetables to perfection. This masala enhances the flavor of any vegetable dish, making it irresistible. It contains turmeric, chili powder, and a medley of aromatic spices that are particularly suited for Indian vegetable curries. Versatile enough to be used in stir-fries and sautéed veggies, it’s an essential staple for health-conscious cooking. Ideal for both routine family meals and impressive dinner parties.

Royal Garam Masala

An exotic mix for those who want to elevate their cooking. This royal version includes premium spices like saffron and nutmeg, perfect for special occasions. Traditionally used in rich Indian dishes, it’s essential for biryanis and special curries. A few pinches can enhance the depth of your culinary creations, giving them an aromatic twist. This blend is sure to impress guests and elevate everyday meals to royal status.

Shahi Garam Masala

Your go-to spice for rich, royal dishes that impress. Inspired by Mughlai flavors, it includes unique spices that bring a regal touch to any meal. Often used in rich gravies and biryanis, this masala is designed to create unforgettable dishes. A little goes a long way, making it both economical and effective in enhancing flavors. Use it for special occasions or to add luxury to weekday dinners.

Chhole Masala

The authentic flavor for traditional chickpea curry. This masala brings together a perfect mix of spices that complement the high-protein chickpeas beautifully. It contains a balance of heat and earthy flavors, ideally suited for various styles of chhole. Perfect for pairing with rice, naan, or bhature, it’s versatile and satisfying. Rich in protein and flavor, this dish is a favorite in North Indian households.
